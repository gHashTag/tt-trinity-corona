## Research (this loop)

A Fibonacci LFSR is maximal-length iff its tap polynomial is primitive over GF(2);
for degree 16 the textbook primitive polynomial is x^16+x^14+x^13+x^11+1 (Xilinx
XAPP052 taps 16,14,13,11). The RTL's `state[15]^[13]^[12]^[10]` is exactly that
polynomial in 0-indexed form, which is why the empirical period is the full
2^16-1. The relevance to the audit: a wrong tap set would not be caught by a few
sample outputs (a non-primitive LFSR still looks random for a while) -- only the
period/coverage test exposes it, the same way the missing-XOR in blake3 was only
exposed by comparing against the real G(). The reference, not the eyeball, is what
catches these. The Cassini-Lucas identity used by the POST,
L_n*L_{n+1} - L_{n-1}*L_{n+2} = 5*(-1)^n, is a one-line consequence of
L_{n+/-1} = L_{n+1} -/+ L_n and holds for n=2..5 as the header claims.
