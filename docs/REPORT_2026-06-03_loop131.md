## Research (this loop, web available)

The deliverable is communication, not computation: a respin go/no-go needs the
defect, its workload-level magnitude, the blast radius (which dies), and the
remediation, in one place -- and equally a list of what was checked and cleared, so
the reader can bound the audit's completeness rather than wonder what else might
lurk. Recording the negatives (gfN off-path, converters dead, Corona clean) is as
much a part of a credible review as the positives. The staging gap is the
shared-codegen lesson once more: a fix lives where you put it, not where the bug is;
the bug fanned to three dies via one generator, so the fix has to be fanned back the
same way before the brief can honestly claim every affected die is covered.
