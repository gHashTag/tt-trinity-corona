## Research (this loop)

The WIDTHEXPAND vs WIDTHTRUNC distinction is the crux of why this check matters and
why it came back clean. Width EXPANSION (assigning a narrow value into a wider lvalue)
is a safe, lossless sign/zero extension -- the synthesizer pads the high bits. Width
TRUNCATION (a value wider than its destination) silently drops high bits and is the
exact archetype of this whole audit's defects (gf16_mul mantissa, bitnet neuron base,
gf_formats IDs -- "a field one bit too narrow"). A linter that reports both as
"WIDTH..." can mask the difference; the project's `width_gate.py` deliberately gates
only the truncation/out-of-range-select class, which is why it stays green on the v2
fixes (they only widen) while still catching the harmful pattern. The fixes adding
WIDTHEXPAND notes is expected -- e.g. bitnet_encoder_v2 widens the neuron address
path, which is the fix.
