## Research (this loop, web available)

Two lessons. First, the degenerate-format trap: GF4's bias of 0 makes the implicit
"1.0 = exp=bias" coincide with the zero code, so 1.0 is unrepresentable and the
generic exponent probe tests nonsense -- the fix was to recognise the degeneracy
and switch to an exhaustive truth-table check (feasible at 4 bits), not to force
the probe. Second, a verified unit is only verified against what it was tested on:
gf16_add passed its FPGA/MNIST validation yet the exact-rational sweep shows it is
not faithfully rounded (truncating alignment, Goldberg / Muller et al. on why GRS
bits are mandatory). The sweep -- an independent oracle -- caught what the original
test campaign did not, which is the whole point of an independent reference. The
generator-self-validation pattern (regenerate a known-good unit, re-run its
exhaustive test) is a cheap, strong guard that the code-gen is faithful before
trusting it on a new rung.

### Option A: Bring gf16_add onto the guard/round/sticky path (close the last imprecision)
gf16_add is the only remaining inaccurate add (the silicon-`[Verified]` rung,
~512 ULP on cancellation). `gen_gf_add_fix.py` already generalizes to (E=6,M=9);
regenerate gf16_add, verify with a gf16 add sweep (add gf16 back to
`gf_add_sweep.py`), and confirm the canonical anchor test still passes. This makes
the entire add family faithfully rounded. Needs a nod that re-touching the
silicon-validated rung is in scope. **Recommended -- it is the one real accuracy
gap left.**

### Option B: Audit + fix the gfN special-value encodings
Several rungs carry NaN/Inf constants that look inconsistent (an "Inf" with a
nonzero mantissa field, which the unit's own `is_inf` test -- `mant==0` -- would
classify as NaN, so a produced Inf would not round-trip). Cross-check every rung's
special encodings against the canonical t27 spec and the decode path, and align
them. Conformance cleanup orthogonal to the (now-correct) datapath.

### Option C: Resolve the GF256 bias open conjecture
GF256 is the one rung excluded from the cross-check because its stored exponent
bias does not reconcile with `2^(E-1)-1`. Pin the actual convention from the t27
spec / decode RTL, document it, and bring GF256 arithmetic under the same probe +
sweep. Highest research content, lowest certainty of a clean result.
