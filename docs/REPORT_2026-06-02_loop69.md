### MX-Format Hardware Converter (New Reference)

- **Gorodecky & Sousa** (arXiv:2411.03149, Nov 2024): FPGA-implemented converter supporting 6 MX sub-formats (E5M2, E4M3, E3M2, E2M3, E2M1, INT8). Memory-free design. **Closest architectural reference** to Corona's multi-format approach — but FPGA-only and 6 formats vs Corona's 80.
- **2017 Unum ALU ASIC** (arXiv:1712.01021, 65nm): Only prior taped-out ASIC with format conversion as first-class function. Corona extends this concept to 80 formats.

### Open-Source Verification Innovation

- **Normal Computing CIRCT simulator** (Jan-Feb 2026): 580K lines added to LLVM CIRCT via AI agents in 43 days. Includes event-driven Verilog simulator with JIT, VPI bridge + cocotb integration, UVM runtime, BMC, LEC, and mutation testing. Potentially bridges pre-silicon and post-silicon verification with same Python testbenches.

### Novelty Confirmation (Final)

No prior "format oracle" or "format conformance checker" ASIC exists in published literature. Corona is confirmed novel across: (1) ROM-based format catalogs, (2) multi-format decode hardware, (3) silicon-level conformance testing. The MX converter paper and Unum ALU are the closest architectural predecessors.
