## Research (this loop, web available)

The ternary popcount-MAC is the standard BitNet/ternary-net inner-product trick: with
weights/activations in {-1,0,+1}, a dot product reduces to (matches) - (mismatches)
over the active (both-nonzero) lanes, i.e. two popcounts and a subtract -- no
multiplier (hence the R-SI-1 "no `*`" compliance). Verifying it is cheap and total in
spirit: the result is a small integer in [-N, N], the per-lane logic is a 2-bit decode,
and a few thousand random vectors exercise the popcount tree and the pipeline ordering
thoroughly. The useful outcome is the completed coverage statement: the shipped silicon
has exactly two compute paths, and both are now functionally checked -- the float MAC
(defective, fixed) and the ternary MAC (clean).
