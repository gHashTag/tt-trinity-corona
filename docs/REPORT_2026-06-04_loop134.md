## Why

After characterizing and impact-measuring the defect and staging per-die v2 fixes,
the one thing left that actually prevents recurrence is fixing the upstream source.
A symptom fix in each die doesn't help the next die; the master does. Finding that
`t27/rtl_gen/gf16_mul.v` is the master (and is itself buggy, identical to Euler's
shipped copy) made the root-cause fix concrete.

## Research (this loop, web available)

The arc's recurring theme lands here: a shared code-gen / shared-source pipeline
means a defect has exactly one root and many leaves, and durable remediation is at
the root -- patching leaves (the per-die v2 modules) protects today's consumers but
not tomorrow's. The discipline is to fix the master and re-verify the master against
an independent oracle (here the exhaustively-validated v2, 0 diffs over 1.8M pairs),
not to trust that "the same change applied upstream" is correct by analogy. It also
shows the value of carrying provenance in file headers: the `t27/rtl_gen/...` path in
every die's copy is what made the master trivially findable -- traceability paid off
exactly when a root-cause fix was needed.
