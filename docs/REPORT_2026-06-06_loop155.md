## Research (this loop)

The result is a clean instance of the difference between a SIGNAL-level error metric
and a DECISION-level one. At the logit/ULP level the defect is everywhere -- 73% of
accumulations differ between shipped and corrected -- because the two gf16 rounding
paths disagree by a ULP on most 16-term sums. At the argmax/decision level it is
nearly invisible (0.13%), because a trained softmax classifier separates classes by
margins far larger than a ULP; the sparse halving error only matters in the thin band
where the top two logits are within the perturbation of each other, and training
actively widens that band away from the data. This is why the synthetic random
classifier (no training -> many near-ties) over-stated the impact at 0.24%, and a
trained model lands lower. The general lesson for quantization-defect triage: measure
at the decision boundary the product actually makes, not only at the arithmetic
output -- an 11x RMS MAC error and a 0.13% decision-flip rate are both true of the
same defect.
